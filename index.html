About Me

Throughout secondary school, I noticed that my year group naturally formed a hierarchy. As the hierarchy would shift, different people would rise and fall within the structures. Although these shifts were sometimes unpleasant experiences, I began questioning certain cognition and behaviours that I had previously accepted. I was eager to understand how brain functions impact daily behaviours. 

I now aspire to investigate the neural foundations of behaviour, examine the causes and effects of developmental or acquired neuropsychological deficits and have a greater understanding of the relationship between the brain and behaviour. Due to this, I am excited about the prospect of studying this Masters program in Behavioural Neuroscience.

I am currently working as a Psychology Project Worker in WALK, a disability day-service. Understanding how people with intellectual disabilities perceive their environment and compensate for their sensory deficits fascinates me. This experience has piqued my interest in exploring the interaction between the environment and brain function.
